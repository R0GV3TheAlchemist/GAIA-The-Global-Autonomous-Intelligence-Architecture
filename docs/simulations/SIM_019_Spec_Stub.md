## What This Simulation Is

SIM-019 characterises GAIA’s governance dynamics — how the system maintains stability while remaining adaptive across changing conditions. The governing principle is edge-of-chaos criticality: the hypothesis that GAIA’s governance is most effective when operating near but not at the boundary between ordered and chaotic behaviour.

**Input:** System state descriptors across all active bands — performance headroom, bottleneck severity, phase transition signals
**Output:** Governance stability metric — probability that GAIA correctly prioritises the right band, simulation, or resource allocation decision under varying load conditions

## The Edge-of-Chaos Hypothesis (PRED-012)

GAIA’s governance dynamics are predicted to be most stable at approximately 15–20% below the theoretical performance ceiling across active bands. This “headroom zone” allows:
- Adaptive response to unexpected bottlenecks without destabilisation
- Phase transition capacity without interrupting active optimisation passes
- Recursive improvement without compounding instability

SIM-019 Pass 1 will establish whether the headroom zone exists empirically, where it sits, and what happens to governance stability when the system operates outside it.
