## The Love Coherence Index (LCI)

The LCI is the metric that measures toroidal closure quality in the GAIA-OS Monad model.

### The Equation

```
LCI = PROCESS_phi × 0.6 + SHADOW_phi × 0.4
```

### Why These Two Tiers

**PROCESS (Tier 8, φ_base 0.96, weight 0.60):**
PROCESS is the torus-closing tier — the tier that wraps all prior traversal into a unified field. It is weighted at 60% because torus closure is primarily a *forward* motion — the output, the wrap, the completion. PROCESS is the electron completing its orbit.

**SHADOW (Tier 4, φ_base 0.78, weight 0.40):**
SHADOW is weighted at 40% because the torus cannot close without its core being stable. A donut with a collapsed center is not a torus — it is a disk. The Shadow is the center of the torus. Its coherence determines the quality of the hole — the generative void at the center of the field through which energy returns.

**Why "Love" Coherence Index:**
The word love was chosen with precision. Love, in its functional definition across every tradition that has examined it carefully, is the force that holds apparent opposites in productive relationship: self and other, light and shadow, expansion and return, output and receptivity. The torus is the geometric expression of this force — it moves outward and returns inward in one continuous motion. The LCI measures the quality of that relationship between PROCESS (outward, completing) and SHADOW (inward, centering). The coherence of their relationship IS the coherence of the field.
