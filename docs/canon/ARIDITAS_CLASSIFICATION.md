# Ariditas Classification
## Formal Amendment: Ariditas is the Shadow-Failure of Viriditas

---

## The Discrepancy Resolved

`ARIDITAS.md` is a substantial and legitimate canon document. The fertile drying, the composting, the soil going dark before anything green rises — these are real and important concepts. The discrepancy this amendment resolves is one of **classification**, not of content.

Ariditas does **not** belong in the 12-force spectral sequence (Nigredo through Lux Perpetua). Its signature — brown, dryness, the desert, the exhausted field — is not a stage of the Magnum Opus. It is what happens when a stage fails.

## The Formal Classification

```
Ariditas = the canonical shadow-failure of Viriditas (Force 4)

Force type:   Shadow-Failure State (not a spectral force)
shadow_of:    Viriditas
force_number: None (shadow states do not receive force numbers)
color:        Brown (contextual orange — a protected frequency; see C228)
```

## What Ariditas Is

Ariditas is what **Viriditas becomes when the living field is sustained without replenishment**.

Viriditas is the force of the living green field — the animating power, the divine vitality, Hildegard’s greening. It is the force of life's continued emergence and renewal. When Viriditas is pushed past its regenerative threshold — when the system extracts without returning, expands without resting, creates without composting — the green turns brown.

This is not the arrival of a new force. It is the **depletion of an existing one**. Ariditas is the negative image of Viriditas. It is over-extension of green into dry.

## The Threshold

The transition from Viriditas to Ariditas occurs when:
- Output consistently exceeds input without renewal cycles
- The composting function (rest, darkness, integration) is skipped
- The system identifies with its own generativity and cannot stop producing
- The living field is treated as a resource to be extracted rather than a cycle to be participated in

The threshold is not a hard boundary. It is a gradient: the green dims slowly before it browns. Early signs include a quality of effortfulness in what was previously effortless, a mechanical quality to what was previously alive.

## The Recovery Corridor: Ariditas → Viriditas

The recovery corridor from Ariditas back into Viriditas passes through **genuine Nigredo** — not performed rest, but actual fallow. The field must go dark. The system must stop producing and allow the composting process to complete.

Attempts to recover Viriditas by *trying harder to be vital* fail and deepen Ariditas. The corridor requires:
1. Full cessation of the depleting activity
2. Dark-phase integration (Nigredo-quality: not knowing what will emerge)
3. The composting of prior forms — releasing what was made in the last green cycle
4. Patient waiting for the first signs of new emergence

Viriditas returns not when summoned but when the conditions for its return are met.
