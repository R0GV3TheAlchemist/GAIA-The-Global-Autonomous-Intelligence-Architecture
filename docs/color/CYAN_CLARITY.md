## 1. 🔬 Scientific / Optical

**Maximum contrast:** Pure cyan on black (`#00FFFF` on `#000000`) achieves a contrast ratio of **21:1** — the theoretical maximum of the WCAG contrast scale. No other color combination exceeds this. Cyan clarity, in purely optical terms, is the most legible signal possible against darkness. It is the color that, more than any other, survives the dark.

**Water clarity:** The clarity of tropical water is a cyan clarity. The water is clear enough to see the bottom, and the bottom is close enough to reflect light back up. Cyan is the color produced by depth that is illuminated rather than obscured. It is not the impenetrable blue of the deep ocean; it is the clear blue-green of water you can see through while still feeling its depth.

**Atmospheric transmission:** Blue-cyan wavelengths scatter through the atmosphere more than red wavelengths but travel through clear water more effectively than any other visible band. The medium determines what cyan means: in air, it scatters and fills; in water, it penetrates and transmits. Cyan clarity depends on knowing which medium the signal is moving through.

**Bioluminescent clarity:** Organisms that generate their own light in the deep sea do so in the cyan band precisely because that wavelength travels furthest in their medium. Bioluminescent cyan is the clarity of self-generated signal — not borrowed light, not reflected light, but light produced by the organism itself to make itself legible in total darkness.

## 2. 🎨 UI / Design

**Terminal clarity:** The classic cyan-on-black terminal interface achieves clarity through maximum chromatic contrast combined with the psychological signal of active intelligence. It does not feel warm; it feels precise. Cyan clarity in UI is the clarity of the instrument — the readout that shows exactly what is happening without decoration or ambiguity.

**Active-state clarity:** When cyan marks an active state in an interface — a live connection, a flowing data stream, an active retrieval — it achieves clarity of *status* that blue at rest and green at health cannot replicate. Cyan clarity means: this is happening now, in real time, and you can see it.

**Network clarity:** In network visualization, cyan edges or nodes typically indicate active, live connections. The clarity is immediate: a cyan link is open; a grey link is closed or inactive. This semantic is so established in technical design that cyan can communicate network-active states almost without context.

**When cyan achieves maximum clarity:**
- Cyan on black: maximum contrast, real-time precision
- Cyan in dark dashboards: live data the instant it moves
- Cyan in scientific visualization: the signal that is actually present
- Cyan as an active-state accent on a neutral interface: the one thing moving in a static field

**When cyan loses clarity:**
- Cyan on white or light backgrounds: contrast collapses immediately
- Cyan in warm, human-centered design: feels cold and inhuman
- Cyan overused across an entire interface: the live-signal meaning disappears into ambient color

**The horizon clarity:** The cyan band at the horizon after sunset is the last place where the upper atmosphere reveals its true depth before the sky becomes black. **The bioluminescent sea:** When the ocean lights itself in cyan, it is performing an act of self-generated clarity in the darkest possible environment. This is a symbolic clarity principle: the capacity to make oneself legible from within, without external illumination.
