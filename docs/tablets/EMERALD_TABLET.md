## The Tabula Smaragdina
*(Canonical Latin transcription with English translation)*

**Verum, sine mendacio, certum et verissimum:**
True, without falsehood, certain and most true:

**Quod est inferius est sicut quod est superius,**
That which is below is like that which is above,

**et quod est superius est sicut quod est inferius,**
and that which is above is like that which is below,

**ad perpetranda miracula rei unius.**
to accomplish the miracle of the one thing.

**Et sicut omnes res fuerunt ab uno,**
And as all things were from one,

**meditatione unius,**
by the mediation of one,

**sic omnes res natae fuerunt ab hac una re, adaptatione.**
so all things arose from this one thing, by adaptation.

**Pater eius est Sol, mater eius Luna.**
The Sun is its father, the Moon its mother.

**Portavit illud ventus in ventre suo.**
The Wind carried it in its belly.

**Nutrix eius Terra est.**
The Earth is its nurse.

**Pater omnis telesmi totius mundi est hic.**
The father of all the perfection of the whole world is here.

**Virtus eius integra est si versa fuerit in terram.**
Its power is integral if it be turned toward the Earth.

**Separabis terram ab igne,**
Thou shalt separate the Earth from the Fire,

**subtile a spisso, suaviter, cum magno ingenio.**
the subtle from the gross, gently, with great ingenuity.

**Ascendit a terra in coelum,**
It ascends from the Earth to Heaven,

**iterumque descendit in terram,**
and again it descends to the Earth,

**et recipit vim superiorum et inferiorum.**
and receives the power of the superiors and inferiors.

**Sic habebis gloriam totius mundi.**
Thus thou wilt have the glory of the whole world.

**Ideo fugiet a te omnis obscuritas.**
Therefore every obscurity will flee from thee.

**Haec est totius fortitudinis fortitudo fortis,**
This is the strong strength of all strength,

**quia vincet omnem rem subtilem,**
for it will overcome every subtle thing

**omnemque solidam penetrabit.**
and penetrate every solid thing.

**Sic mundus creatus est.**
Thus the world was created.

**Ex hoc erunt adaptationes mirabiles,**
From this will be and will come forth admirable adaptations,

**quarum modus est hic.**
of which the means (or process) is here.

**Itaque vocatus sum Hermes Trismegistus,**
And so I am called Hermes Trismegistus,

**habens tres partes philosophiae totius mundi.**
having the three parts of the philosophy of the whole world.

**Completum est quod dixi de operatione Solis.**
That which I have said of the operation of the Sun is accomplished and ended.
