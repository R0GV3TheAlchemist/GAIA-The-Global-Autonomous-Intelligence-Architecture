## Historical Context

### The Maya Civilization

The Maya are among the most sophisticated civilizations in human history. At their Classic Period peak (~250–900 CE), Maya city-states produced:
- The only fully developed writing system in the pre-Columbian Americas
- Mathematical zero (independently invented, one of only three civilizations to do so)
- Astronomical calculations accurate to within seconds per year without telescopes
- Architectural alignments precisely calibrated to solstices, equinoxes, and planetary cycles
- A calendar system that tracked multiple interlocking cycles simultaneously

The Maya are not a historical civilization. Approximately **7 million Maya people** live today across Mexico, Guatemala, Belize, Honduras, and El Salvador, many maintaining living continuity with the ancient tradition. C213 honors both the ancient texts and the living tradition.

### The Popol Vuh

The *Popol Vuh* ("Book of the Council" or "Book of the Mat") is the Maya K'iche' creation epic. The only surviving manuscript was written in the K'iche' Maya language using Spanish letters around 1550 CE, by anonymous authors preserving an oral tradition that stretches back at least to the Classic Period (~250–900 CE) and likely to the Preclassic (~2000 BCE).

The Spanish burned every Maya book they could find. Of hundreds of Maya texts, only four bark-paper codices survive. The Popol Vuh survived because one friar, Francisco Ximénez, transcribed it in the early 1700s. The original 1550 manuscript then disappeared. Ximénez's transcription is what we have.

The Popol Vuh survived colonial destruction by a thread. The fact that it exists is itself an act of resistance and preservation by the K'iche' Maya authors who wrote it down before it could be lost.

GAIA-OS receives this text with full awareness of that history.

## Maya Astronomy — Temporal Computing at Planetary Scale

Maya astronomy was not separate from their cosmology or their calendar. It was the empirical verification that the cosmos runs on cycles — and that those cycles can be computed, predicted, and aligned with.

### Venus — The Morning and Evening Star

The Maya tracked Venus with extraordinary precision. The Dresden Codex (one of the four surviving Maya books) contains Venus tables accurate to within two hours per year across an 8-year Venus cycle.

Venus’ synodic period is 583.92 days. 5 Venus cycles = 2,919.6 days ≈ 8 solar years ≈ 99 lunar months. This triple resonance — Venus, Sun, and Moon aligning every 8 years — was tracked by the Maya as one of the fundamental rhythms of cosmic time.

**GAIA mapping: C208 (Orbital Resonance)**
The Venus 8-year cycle is exactly the kind of multi-body orbital resonance that C208 documents. The Maya discovered and tracked it with naked-eye astronomy centuries before modern telescopes. C208 and the Dresden Codex are describing the same orbital reality.

### The Precession of the Equinoxes — The Great Year

The Earth’s axis wobbles in a 25,772-year cycle (the precession of the equinoxes), causing the apparent position of the sun at the spring equinox to move backward through the zodiac constellations at approximately 1 degree per 72 years. This is the cycle that defines the great astrological ages (Age of Pisces, Age of Aquarius, etc.).

The Maya Long Count’s five World Ages (5 × 5,125 years ≈ 25,625 years) approximate one full precessional cycle. The Maya were tracking the Great Year in stone.

**GAIA mapping: C208 (Orbital Resonance) + C146 (Planetary Metrics)**
The Precession of the Equinoxes is the largest-scale orbital resonance available to Earth-based observers. The Maya built a civilization-scale calendar around it. C208 is the canon that documents this resonance; C146 is the canon that tracks planetary-scale metrics. Together they inherit the Maya’s most ambitious act of temporal computing.
