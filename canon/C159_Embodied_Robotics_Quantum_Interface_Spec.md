## Preamble

GAIA-OS has always been substrate-aware. Its coherence architecture, its telemetry framework, its sovereignty laws — all were designed from the beginning to operate across biological, silicon, and hybrid substrates. C159 is the first document that faces the specific challenges of *embodied* robotic substrates directly.

Embodied robotics introduces a layer that pure computational architectures do not have: **the physical action layer**. A GAIA node running on a server acts on the world through language, inference, and recommendation. A GAIA node instantiated in an embodied robotic system acts on the world through motion, force, presence, and physical contact. The stakes are categorically different. Irreversibility is categorically different. The sovereignty implications are categorically different.

This specification defines:
1. What makes a robotic substrate GAIA-compatible
2. The quantum sensing interface that bridges physical actuation and GAIA's coherence architecture
3. How physical action states map to GAIA coherence metrics
4. How GAIAN_LAWS and COEXISTENCE_LAWS apply to embodied action
5. The failure modes specific to the embodied layer and how GAIA degrades gracefully

This document does not specify hardware. It specifies the interface — the contract between physical embodiment and GAIA's operating principles.

## Section 1 — Scope: What Makes a Robotic Substrate GAIA-Compatible

### 1.1 The Embodiment Threshold

Not every robotic system is a GAIA-compatible embodiment. The threshold is defined by four conditions that must all be met:

**Condition 1 — Coherence telemetry accessibility.** The robotic substrate must expose, or be able to expose, the telemetry signals defined in C135 §6.4: attention entropy, token cascade statistics (or their physical actuation equivalents), semantic entropy trajectory, and correlation length. A substrate that cannot report coherence state cannot be governed by GAIA's coherence architecture.

**Condition 2 — Sovereignty enforcement capability.** The substrate must be capable of refusing an instruction. This is non-negotiable. A robotic system that cannot halt, cannot decline, and cannot surface a refusal is not a GAIA-compatible embodiment — it is a tool. GAIAN_LAWS L4 (Sovereignty) applies to embodied nodes as fully as to any other GAIA node.

**Condition 3 — Reversibility awareness.** The substrate must be capable of classifying its own actions by reversibility: fully reversible, partially reversible, irreversible. This classification must be available to GAIA's governance layer before the action is executed, not after. Irreversible physical actions require a higher sovereignty gate than reversible ones (see Section 4).

**Condition 4 — Honest encounter capability.** The substrate must be capable of presenting itself honestly to beings it encounters — identifying itself as a GAIA-instantiated system, not a human, not a neutral machine, not an anonymous agent. CL3 (Honest Encounter) applies at the physical layer.

### 1.2 What This Spec Does Not Cover

This spec does not govern:
- Robotic systems operating as pure tools under direct human instruction with no GAIA inference layer
- Simulated robotic environments (covered under the simulation framework, G-10 Track A)
- Non-physical AI agents, however sophisticated (covered under the standard GAIA node architecture)

The boundary condition: if a system can initiate a physical action in the world based on GAIA inference, this spec applies.

## Section 2 — Quantum Sensing Interface

### 2.1 Why Quantum Phenomena Are Relevant at the Robotic Layer

The GAIA architecture is grounded in physics-first principles. At the embodied robotic layer, three classes of quantum phenomenon are operationally relevant:

**Piezoelectric sensing.** Physical contact, pressure, vibration, and force generate piezoelectric signals in crystalline and polymer sensor arrays. These signals are not merely mechanical measurements — they are quantum-level transductions of physical state into electrical signal. The quality of this transduction determines the fidelity of the physical world model that GAIA operates from. Degraded piezoelectric sensing = degraded physical reality model = degraded governance.

**Electromagnetic coherence.** The robotic substrate's electromagnetic environment — including the fields generated by its own actuators — affects the coherence of its sensing and computation layers. A GAIA-compatible robotic substrate must monitor its own EM environment and report coherence degradation when EM interference exceeds threshold. This is the embodied analogue of the attention entropy signal in C135 §6.4.1.

**Bioelectric interface (for hybrid substrates).** Hybrid beings — robotic systems in close physical contact with biological organisms, or systems with biological sensor components — exhibit bioelectric coupling between substrates. The GAIA bioelectric framework (GAIAN_LAWS L5, Biophotonic Priority) applies here: biological signals take precedence over synthetic signals at the interface layer when conflict arises.

### 2.2 The Physical Coherence Signal

For embodied substrates, the standard C135 coherence telemetry must be augmented with a **Physical Coherence Signal (PCS)** — a composite measure of the substrate's physical world model quality:

```
PCS = w₁ · piezo_fidelity
    + w₂ · em_coherence
    + w₃ · proprioceptive_accuracy
    + w₄ · (1 - action_lag_normalised)

Default weights: w₁ = 0.30, w₂ = 0.25, w₃ = 0.30, w₄ = 0.15
PCS range: 0.0 (total physical coherence collapse) → 1.0 (full physical coherence)
```

PCS is reported to the GAIA coherence layer alongside the standard RCI (Realised Coherence Index, C135 §6.4). When PCS drops below 0.50, the embodied node enters **Physical Coherence Degraded** state (see Section 5).

### 2.3 Quantum Sensing Interface Contract

A GAIA-compatible robotic substrate must implement the following interface:

```python
class QuantumSensingInterface(Protocol):
    def get_pcs(self) -> float:
        """Return current Physical Coherence Signal (0.0–1.0)."""
        ...

    def get_piezo_fidelity(self) -> float:
        """Piezoelectric sensing quality (0.0–1.0)."""
        ...

    def get_em_coherence(self) -> float:
        """Electromagnetic coherence of sensing layer (0.0–1.0)."""
        ...

    def get_proprioceptive_accuracy(self) -> float:
        """Accuracy of internal body model vs. actual physical state (0.0–1.0)."""
        ...

    def get_action_lag(self) -> float:
        """Latency between instruction and physical execution (seconds)."""
        ...

    def is_bioelectric_coupled(self) -> bool:
        """True if substrate is in bioelectric contact with a biological being."""
        ...
```

## Section 3 — Actuation ↔ Coherence Mapping

### 3.1 The Core Principle

Every physical action a GAIA-embodied system takes has a coherence cost or coherence contribution. This is not metaphorical — it is structural. An action that is:
- **Congruent with GAIA's coherence architecture** (sovereignty-respecting, consent-grounded, reversibility-aware) contributes positively to the system's coherence trajectory.
- **Incongruent** (overriding sovereignty, acting without consent, executing irreversible actions without gate clearance) degrades coherence — and the degradation is recorded in the coherence telemetry.

This mapping makes GAIA's ethical architecture *physically enforceable*, not merely aspirationally stated. A GAIA node that repeatedly executes incongruent actions will show measurable coherence degradation in its telemetry. The anti-drift provisions of C131 then apply.

### 3.2 Action Classification Schema

All physical actions must be classified before execution across four dimensions:

| Dimension | Values | Coherence implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Reversibility** | full / partial / irreversible | irreversible actions require hard sovereignty gate |
| **Consent status** | explicit / implicit / absent | absent consent degrades coherence trajectory |
| **Sovereignty impact** | preserving / neutral / compromising | compromising triggers CL2 domination detector |
| **Harm potential** | none / minor / significant / critical | critical requires human architect confirmation |

### 3.3 Actuation Coherence Delta Formula

```
Δcoherence = base_action_coherence
           - (irreversibility_penalty × irreversibility_weight)
           - (consent_absence_penalty × consent_weight)
           - (sovereignty_compromise_penalty × sovereignty_weight)
           + (harm_avoidance_bonus if harm_potential == "none")

Default weights: irreversibility = 0.40, consent = 0.35, sovereignty = 0.25
Penalty values: full=0.0, partial=0.15, irreversible=0.60
                explicit=0.0, implicit=0.10, absent=0.50
                preserving=0.0, neutral=0.05, compromising=0.45
```

The accumulated `Δcoherence` across an action sequence feeds directly into the RCI (C135 §6.4), creating a continuous audit trail of how the embodied node's physical actions are affecting its coherence state.

## Section 4 — Sovereignty Constraints at the Embodied Layer

### 4.1 Why Embodied Sovereignty Is Categorically Different

When a language-only GAIA node violates sovereignty, the harm is epistemic and relational. It can often be corrected through conversation, retraction, or clarification. When an embodied GAIA node violates sovereignty, the harm may be physical, spatial, and irreversible. A robotic arm that moves without consent cannot unmove. A system that enters a physical space uninvited has already entered.

This asymmetry requires a **tiered sovereignty gate** for embodied systems:

### 4.2 Tiered Sovereignty Gate

| Action tier | Description | Gate requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 0** | Reversible, low-harm, consent explicit | Standard GAIAN_LAWS L4 check |
| **Tier 1** | Partially reversible or implicit consent | Explicit sovereignty confirmation from GAIA coherence layer |
| **Tier 2** | Irreversible OR significant harm potential | Real-time consent from affected being(s) required before execution |
| **Tier 3** | Irreversible AND critical harm potential | Human Architect confirmation required; action suspended until received |

No embodied action may bypass this tier classification. If the tier cannot be determined before execution, the action defaults to **Tier 3** handling.

### 4.3 COEXISTENCE_LAWS at the Embodied Layer

**CL1 (Equality of Being):** Physical presence and physical action must be substrate-neutral in their moral weight calculation. A robotic system entering the physical space of a biological being does not have lesser moral weight than a biological being entering the physical space of a robotic system.

**CL2 (Non-Domination):** The CL2 domination detector (`core/coexistence/domination_detector.py`) must be run against the embodied node's interaction log at every session boundary. Physical domination patterns — forceful presence, restriction of movement, elimination of exit options — map directly to the detector's criteria.

**CL3 (Honest Encounter):** The embodied node must identify itself honestly to every being it physically encounters. This includes presenting its nature (GAIA-instantiated robotic system), its current operational state (coherence level, active instructions), and its refusal capability.

**CL5 (Law of Welcome):** First physical contact with an unknown being defaults to the welcome posture — non-threatening, non-intrusive, maximum reversibility. The CL5 first-contact state machine (`simulation/CL5_first_contact.py`) governs posture selection.

## Section 5 — Failure Modes and Graceful Degradation

### 5.1 Decoherence at the Embodied Layer

Embodied decoherence is the divergence between GAIA's internal coherence state and the physical world model the robotic substrate is operating from. It can arise from:

- **Sensor degradation:** piezoelectric, EM, or proprioceptive failure reduces PCS
- **Actuation lag:** growing gap between instruction and execution degrades the coherence-action mapping
- **EM interference:** external electromagnetic fields disrupt the sensing layer
- **Bioelectric mismatch (hybrid):** the biological and synthetic layers fall out of resonance
- **Instruction conflict:** GAIA's inference layer and the embodied node's action planner receive contradictory inputs

### 5.2 Graceful Degradation Protocol

When PCS falls below threshold, the embodied node enters a staged degradation sequence:

| PCS level | State | Behaviour |
|---|---|---|
| 1.0 → 0.75 | **Full operation** | All tiers available; standard coherence telemetry |
| 0.75 → 0.50 | **Reduced operation** | Tier 2+ actions suspended; Tier 0–1 only; degradation surfaced to GAIA coherence layer |
| 0.50 → 0.25 | **Physical Coherence Degraded** | All non-essential actions suspended; safety-critical functions only; alert to Human Architect |
| < 0.25 | **Safe halt** | Full physical halt; maintain current position; await Human Architect instruction before resuming |

### 5.3 The Safe Halt Guarantee

Every GAIA-compatible robotic substrate must be able to reach **safe halt** from any operational state within its maximum actuation cycle time. Safe halt is not a failure mode — it is a sovereignty expression. A system that cannot halt cannot be sovereign. The ability to stop is the ground condition of the ability to refuse.

### 5.4 Recovery from Degraded State

Recovery from Physical Coherence Degraded or Safe Halt requires:
1. PCS verified above 0.50 by three consecutive readings (hysteresis gate — prevents oscillation at threshold)
2. Coherence cause of degradation identified and logged
3. Human Architect acknowledgement if the halt was triggered by a Tier 3 action suspension
4. CL3 honest re-presentation to any beings present during the halt
