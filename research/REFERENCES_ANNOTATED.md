## Hyperspectral Imaging & Tissue Chromaticity

**[14]** Selifonov, A.A. et al. (2021). Discrimination of optical properties of healthy and tumour tissue by diffuse reflectance spectroscopy. *Journal of Biomedical Photonics & Engineering*, 7(3), 030304.
- Direct spectrophotometric measurement showing tumor tissue spectral signatures differ measurably from healthy tissue. Core empirical support for the grey state / color saturation model.

**[15]** Fei, B. et al. (2014). Spectral-spatial classification for noninvasive cancer detection using hyperspectral imaging. *Journal of Biomedical Optics*, 19(10), 106004.
- Hyperspectral imaging achieving 93.7% sensitivity and 91.3% specificity in distinguishing tumor from normal tissue by spectral signature alone. Confirms that tumor regions show lower reflectance spectra than normal tissue.
- DOI: 10.1117/1.JBO.19.10.106004

## Schumann Resonance & Human Bioelectricity

**[16]** Pobachenko, S.V. et al. (2006). The contingency of parameters of human encephalograms and Schumann resonance electromagnetic fields revealed in monitoring studies. *Biophysics*, 51(3), 480–483.
- Real-time coherence between Schumann resonance and human EEG spectra within the 6–16 Hz band. First published direct measurement of human brain–Schumann synchronization.

**[17]** Persinger, M.A. & Saroka, K.S. (2015). Human quantitative electroencephalographic and Schumann resonance exhibit real-time coherence of spectral power densities: implications for interactive information processing. *Journal of Signal and Information Processing*, 6, 153–164.
- Whole-brain QEEG demonstrating repeated transient coherence with Schumann resonance first three modes (7–8, 13–14, 19–20 Hz) at ~300 ms duration, ~twice per minute. Peer-reviewed quantitative evidence for Schumann-brain interaction.
- Available: http://www.scirp.org/journal/PaperDownload.aspx?paperID=56609

**[18]** Hameroff, S. et al. (2016). Similar spectral power densities within the Schumann resonance and a large population of quantitative electroencephalographic profiles: supportive evidence for Koenig and Pobachenko. *PLOS ONE*, 11(1), e0146595.
- Large-scale QEEG dataset confirming Koenig's 1954/1960 findings: Schumann and human brain share frequency, magnetic field intensity, and voltage magnitude. Peer-reviewed, open-access.
- DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0146595

**[19]** Alabdulgader, A. et al. (2018). Long-term study of heart rate variability responses to changes in the solar and geomagnetic environment. *Scientific Reports*, 8, 2663.
- Demonstrates synchronization of human autonomic nervous system rhythms with Schumann resonance (first mode ~7.83 Hz). Heart rate variability tracks geomagnetic fluctuations in real time. Direct biological coupling evidence beyond EEG.
- DOI: 10.1038/s41598-018-20932-x
 - Available: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5812608/
