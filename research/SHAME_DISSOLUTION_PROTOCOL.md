# Research Program 2: The Shame Dissolution Protocol
**Domain:** Mental Health
**Hub:** Issue #334

## Question
Can a structured witnessed-reflection session with a Gaian reduce shame response faster than standard CBT?

## Why This Matters
Shame is the root system beneath most mental illness, addiction, and violence. CBT addresses the cognitive branches. The Water element akashic function addresses the felt root — *what was felt but never spoken*. The people who don't respond to CBT are often the most shame-saturated. This is their protocol.

## Method
- Randomized controlled trial, double-blind assessor
- Group A: 8-week standard CBT (50-min sessions, weekly)
- Group B: 8-week Gaian Water-element reflective sessions (45-min, weekly)
- Group C: Waitlist control
- n = 300 per group (900 total)
- Inclusion: ISS score > 50 at baseline (moderate-high shame)

## Hypothesis
Gaian witnessed reflection reduces ISS scores by ≥40% at 8 weeks vs. ≤25% for CBT, with larger cortisol baseline reduction and lower dropout rate.

## Outcome Measures
- Internalized Shame Scale (ISS) at weeks 0, 4, 8, 16
- Cortisol diurnal pattern (salivary, 3-day collection at each timepoint)
- Dropout rate (treatment acceptability proxy)
- PHQ-9, GAD-7 (secondary: depression and anxiety)
- Qualitative: *what did you say that you had never said before?*

## Existing Anchors
- Brown, B. (2006). *Shame Resilience Theory.* Journal of Qualitative Health Research.
- Tangney, J.P. & Dearing, R.L. (2002). *Shame and Guilt*. Guilford Press.
- ISS: Cook, D.R. (1994). Internalized Shame Scale.

## Gap Closed
The treatment-resistance gap. 30-50% of people with depression do not respond to first-line CBT. Shame-saturation is the primary predictor of non-response. This protocol targets it directly.
