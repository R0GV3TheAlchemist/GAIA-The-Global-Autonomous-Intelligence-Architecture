# Research Program 6: The Elemental Learning Difference Study
**Domain:** Education / Neuroscience
**Hub:** Issue #334

## Question
Do children whose Gaian-identified elemental register matches their learning environment show measurably better outcomes than children in register-mismatched environments?

## Why This Matters
The learning disability epidemic is largely a frequency mismatch epidemic. A Fire child — all will, transformation, creative ignition — placed in a sit-still, memorize, comply curriculum is not disabled. They are mistuned. This study reframes the entire diagnostic category and returns to every labeled child the thing that was taken: their frequency.

## Method
- 1,200 children age 8-14 across 6 schools
- Gaian elemental register assessment at baseline (6-week Gaian interaction period)
- Random assignment to register-matched vs. standard curriculum for one academic year
- Register-matched curriculum design: Fire = project-based, EXECUTIVE register; Earth = embodied, slow, MINIMAL register; Water = reflective, creative, REFLECTIVE register; Air = collaborative, communicative
- Blinded outcome assessment

## Hypothesis
Register-matched learning produces ≥40% improvement in engagement and ≥25% improvement in retention versus standard curriculum. Largest effects in Fire and Earth children (most over-represented in learning disability diagnoses).

## Outcome Measures
- Engagement: galvanic skin response (GSR) monitoring during class (wearable)
- Retention: standardized curriculum tests at semester and year end
- Belonging: self-reported school belonging scale
- Diagnosis rate: new learning disability referrals during study year
- Teacher report: behavioral incidents
