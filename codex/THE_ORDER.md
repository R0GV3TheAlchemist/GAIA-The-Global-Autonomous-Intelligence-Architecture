## The Law

**PROOFS ARE MANDATORY.**

No insight may be treated as canon without a proof.
No theory may be committed to `docs/canon/` without simulation, formal derivation, or empirical grounding.
No session closes without at least one proof committed to the repository.

This is not a guideline. This is not a best practice. This is the order.

---

## What Counts as a Proof

| Type | Definition | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Simulation** | Code executed, results recorded, behavior confirmed across multiple conditions | Metallic Spectrum — 5 trajectories × 60 turns × 8 tiers |
| **Formal Derivation** | Mathematical or logical proof from first principles | Shadow gating function: `φ_post = φ × (0.55 + 0.45 × φ_shadow)` |
| **Empirical** | Observed behavior in the running system, documented with inputs and outputs | Runtime coherence measurements from `gaian_runtime.py` |
| **Emergent Confirmation** | A theory intuited, then independently confirmed by simulation or system behavior | Gold→Platinum theory arrived before simulation; simulation confirmed it |

**What does NOT count as proof:**
- A feeling (however accurate)
- A conversation (however deep)
- A document that says something is true without showing why
- Prior art from external sources without adaptation to the GAIA-OS model

Feelings and conversations are the *source* of proofs. They are the emergence layer. They are not the proof itself. The proof is what you build *after* the emergence.

## The Codex Enforcement Protocol

Every canon document committed to `docs/canon/` MUST contain:

```markdown
## Proof
**Type:** [Simulation | Formal | Empirical | Emergent Confirmation]
**Status:** [Proven | Partial | In Progress]
**Method:** [description of how the proof was conducted]
**Results:** [what the proof showed]
**Committed:** [date and session reference]
```

Any document in `docs/canon/` without this block is **incomplete canon** and must be flagged for proof completion before it can be cited as authoritative.

The `collective/PROOFS_INDEX.md` is the master registry. Every proof must be indexed there.
